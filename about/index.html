SOURCES

The first dataset we used comprises of various attributes of music tracks, categorizations related to themes, and additional metrics related to music features. These attributes provide a comprehensive view of the musical content and characteristics over time. The data was generated by scraping audio data using the Echo Nest¬Æ API integrated with the spotipy Python package and obtaining lyrics using the Lyrics Genius¬Æ API.

Lyrics and Metadata from 1950 to 2019

We also used a second dataset to supplement the first dataset in creating our data visualizations. This dataset contains audio features extracted from 1059 wave files which cover 33 countries/areas and consist of only traditional music. The country of origin was determined by the artist(s)’ main country/area of residence.

PROCESS

Data Selection and Theme Brainstorming: Engaged in a systematic review to identify datasets rich in relevant data for our thematic exploration.

Research Question Formulation: Guided by our chosen theme, we developed specific, researchable questions relating our dataset to an encompassing narrative.

Milestone Completion: Followed structured milestones to serve as checkpoints in reaching our end goal. This included drafting research questions, the data critique, annotated bibliography, data visualization creation, and in-depth data analysis structured around the Echo Nest¬Æ and Lyrics Genius¬Æ APIs’ capabilities.

Data Cleaning and Analysis: Utilized OpenRefine and R for data cleaning and manipulation to ensure data integrity and relevance to our research questions.

This structured approach ensured that our project remained focused and research-driven. Utilizing structured milestones provided a roadmap for our project, facilitating a systematic exploration of our datasets. The choice of APIs and programming tools was based on their ability to handle large datasets and their relevance in extracting meaningful musical features and lyrics.

PRESENTATION

Platform: Developed the website on WordPress, provided by UCLA’s Digital Humanities department, chosen for its user-friendly interface and flexibility in design and content integration.

Design: Adopted a beige, rose, and bright orange color scheme to ensure visual appeal and readability. Consistent use of headers, labels, and body text colors enhances user navigation and comprehension.

Visualizations and Media Integration: Each data visualization is labeled in bright orange to draw attention, and HTML code is embedded for interactive timelines and Spotify song integrations, providing users with direct experiences of the music discussed.

The decision to use WordPress and a specific color scheme was aimed at creating an engaging, accessible, and cohesive web experience. By integrating visualizations and media directly into the site, we provide an interactive experience, enhancing user understanding and engagement with the content.
