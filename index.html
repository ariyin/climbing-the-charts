CLIMBING THE CHARTS

seeing how music has evolved over the years

ABOUT THIS PROJECT

This project explores the evolution of music over seven decades, from 1950 to 2019, using a comprehensive dataset that includes artist names, track names, release dates, genres, lyrics, and various musical features including danceability, energy, and instrumentals. Our goal is to understand how music has transformed in response to societal, cultural, and technological changes.

We chose to observe the music industry because we predict that the music on the charts is not just a product of fleeting trends, but rather a consequence of societal shifts that shape the cultural era. For this project, we plan to analyze these shifts by narrowing the scope of our focus on two main events: the Vietnam War and the advent of technology.

OUR RESEARCH QUESTIONS

How have cultural changes and
major historical events influenced lyrical themes
and music genres from 1950 to 2019?

How did the Vietnam War era influence the themes, lyrics, and styles of music across various genres through the 1950s to the 1970s?

In what ways has technological advancement, particularly in recording, production, distribution, and digital platforms, influenced the creation, consumption, and evolution of music in the 21st century?

PLACE IN LITERATURE

Our research is backed with information from academic journals and articles such as Anglada-Tort et al. (2019), which discusses gender disparities in music, and Christenson et al. (2018), focusing on lyrical themes in U.S. top-40 songs. We also draw upon quantitative analysis such as that in the work of Mauch et al. (2015) on the evolution of popular music. These sources provide crucial context, highlighting how previous research has approached the study of music trends, cultural impacts, and the role of technology.
